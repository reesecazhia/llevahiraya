How to Interpret Disaster Data?

Matuto kung paano uunawain ang mga disaster reports at alerts!

Earthquake

The Magnitude can be recorded by a seismometer. It is the size of an earthquake. It is the energy released by an earthquake at the focus.
The Intensity, on the other hand, is the measure of shaking at each location, and this varies from place to place.

The PHIVOLCS Earthquake Intensity Scale

  • I- Scarcely Perceptible
  • II- Slightly Felt
  • III- Weak
  • IV- Moderately Strong
  • V- Strong
  • VI- Very Strong
  • VII- Destructive
  • VIII- Very Destructive
  • IX- Devastating
  • X- Completely Devastating

Tropical Cyclone

  • 1- Strong Winds: Minimal to minor threat to life and property
  • 2- Gale Force Winds: Minor to moderate threat to life and property
  • 3- Storm-force winds: Moderate to significant threat to life and property
  • 4- Typhoon-force winds: Significant to severe threat to life and property
  • 5- Typhoon-force winds: Extreme threat to life and property

Flood

  • Flood Monitoring: Flood is Possible, Light to Moderate Rainfall
  • Flood Alert: Flood is Threatening, Moderate to Heavy Rainfall
  • Flood Warning: Flood is Occurring, Heavy to Intense Rainfall
  • Severe Flood Warning: Floo is Persisting, Intense to Torrential Rainfall
  • Final: Flood is no longer possible

Rainfall

  • Rainfall Advisory- Flooding is POSSIBLE in low-lying areas and near river channels.
  • Rainfall Alert- Flooding is THREATENING in low-lying areas and near river channels.
  • Rainfall Warning- SEVERE Flooding is EXPECTED. Take necessary precautionary measures.
